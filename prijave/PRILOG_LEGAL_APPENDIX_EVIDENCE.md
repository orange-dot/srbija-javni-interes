### DOKAZ 1: Amnesty International – „Trapped by Automation“

**Bibliografska referenca**  
- Naslov: Trapped by Automation: Poverty and discrimination in Serbia's welfare state  
- Izdavac: Amnesty International  
- Datum publikacije: 4. decembar 2023  
- Tip: međunarodni istrazivacki izveštaj  
- URL: https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/research/2023/12/trapped-by-automation-poverty-and-discrimination-in-serbias-welfare-state/  
- Jezik: engleski  
- Dužina: 40+ strana

**Ključni nalazi**  
1. Implementacija „Socijalne karte“ (mart 2022) isključila 27.000–35.000 ljudi iz sistema socijalne zaštite.  
2. Algoritamska diskriminacija posebno pogodila Rome i osobe sa invaliditetom.  
3. Sistem finansirala Svetska banka sa 82,6 miliona evra.  
4. Nema transparentnosti kriterijuma, nejasan proces zalbe.

**Primeri algoritamskih gresaka**  
- Nasledena imovina tretirana kao dohodak.  
- Pomoć za sahranu tretirana kao redovan prihod.  
- Vozila odavno prodata vodjena kao aktivna imovina.  
- Nije moguce dobiti kopiju izveštaja koji je osnov za odbijanje.

**Direktni citati**  
Za PRIJAVA_I (Sekcija IV.A.1):  
> „The Government's new system, launched on March 1, 2022, removed approximately 27,000 to 35,000 people from the social assistance register within its first 10 months. As a result, 176,000 beneficiaries remained as of August 2023, down from a previous 211,000.“ (Amnesty, 2023)

Za PRIJAVA_III (Sekcija VII.A.2):  
> „People with Roma ethnicity were particularly affected by the system's flawed logic. Individuals reported that inherited property was misclassified as income, funeral relief payments were treated as regular income, and cars registered to family members years ago were flagged as active assets.“ (Amnesty, 2023)

Za PRIJAVA_IV (Sekcija 4.A.1):  
> „The World Bank pre-financed the system with €82.6 million… Despite international funding commitments, the system contained fundamental design flaws that discriminated against vulnerable populations.“ (Amnesty, 2023)

**Pravna valjanost:** vrlo visoka – međunarodna organizacija, javni izveštaj, direktno verifikovan.

### DOKAZ 2: OCCRP – „Serbia: Skeletons in the Closet“

**Bibliografska referenca**  
- Naslov: Serbia: Skeletons in the Closet - How Did the Health Minister get an Apartment  
- Izdavac: OCCRP / KRIK  
- Datum: 21. februar 2016  
- Tip: istrazivacko novinarstvo  
- URL: https://www.occrp.org/en/investigation/serbia-skeletons-in-the-closet-how-did-the-health-minister-get-an-apartment

**Ključni nalazi**  
1. Dr Zlatibor Lončar je 2002. navodno dao smrtonosnu injekciju Veselinu Bozovicu.  
2. Deset dana nakon toga primio stan od Jelene Kalinic (zene Sretka Kalinica).  
3. Clanovi Zemun klana svedocili o njegovom prisustvu na sastancima.  
4. Uprkos navodima, Lončar postao ministar zdravlja.

**Direktni citati**  
Za PRIJAVA_II (Sekcija III.A.1):  
> „He [Lončar] was there every other day at gang headquarters and participated in some murders.“ (OCCRP, 2016)

Za PRIJAVA_II (Sekcija III.A.2):  
> „Lončar purchased a 57-square-meter apartment from Jelena Kalinic for 1.8 million dinars and sold it eight months later for ~€50,000.“ (OCCRP, 2016)

Za PRIJAVA_III (Sekcija VII.A.4):  
> „Despite multiple investigations and gang member testimony, Lončar was appointed Minister of Health in 2014 and reappointed in 2017–2022.“ (OCCRP, 2016)

**Pravna valjanost:** vrlo visoka – međunarodni istrazivacki konzorcijum, svedocenja, imovinski registri.

### DOKAZ 3: Balkan Insight – „Worst Nightmare“ (deinstitucionalizacija)

**Bibliografska referenca**  
- Naslov: 'Worst Nightmare': Serbia's Failure to Deinstitutionalise People with Disabilities  
- Izdavac: Balkan Insight (BIRN)  
- Datum: 25. avgust 2025  
- URL: https://balkaninsight.com/2025/08/25/worst-nightmare-serbias-failure-to-deinstitutionalise-people-with-disabilities/

**Ključni nalazi**  
1. 14.512 osoba u državnim i 8.617 u privatnim institucijama uprkos strategiji deinstitucionalizacije.  
2. Samo 27 osoba koristi usluge zajednice; 4 od 145 opstina imaju podrzano stanovanje.  
3. Vlada priznaje da 40% institucijskih korisnika moze ziveti uz podrsku, ali implementacija stoji.

**Direktni citati**  
Za PRIJAVA_I (Sekcija IV.A.3):  
> „14,512 people remain in state institutions and 8,617 in private facilities… Only 27 individuals with disabilities are using community-based services.“ (Balkan Insight, 2025)

Za PRIJAVA_III (Sekcija VII.A.1):  
> „After eight years, no meaningful progress has been made… implementation has stalled.“ (Balkan Insight, 2025)

Za PRIJAVA_IV (Sekcija 4.A.1):  
> „EU funding for social services remains unimplemented… budgets shifted from community-based services to private institutional placements.“ (Balkan Insight, 2025)

**Pravna valjanost:** vrlo visoka – relevantan izveštaj sa terena, 5 novinara, citiran u EU Progress Report.
