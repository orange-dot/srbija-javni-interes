## VIII. LEGAL BASIS FOR EUROPOL INVESTIGATION

### **Council of Europe Convention on Organized Crime (Palermo Convention):**
> "Organized criminal group means a structured association of three or more persons, established over a period of time, with the aim of committing serious crimes or offenses, for the purpose of obtaining financial or other material benefit."

**Compliance Assessment:** Brnabić–Lončar–Mali network meets all criteria (3+ persons, structured, 11-year duration, serious financial crimes, material benefit documented).

### **EU Convention on Money Laundering (Directive 2015/849/EU):**
> "Conversion or transfer of property, knowing that such property is derived from criminal activity, for the purpose of concealing or disguising the illicit origin of the property."

**Compliance Assessment:** Mali's 45-account network, cross-border transfers, real estate acquisitions constitute systematic money laundering.

### **UNCAC Article 5 (Preventive Measures):**
> "States parties shall establish comprehensive domestic legal frameworks addressing organized corruption affecting public resources."

**Compliance Assessment:** Serbian system failure to prosecute organized network within state institutions = institutional failure to implement UNCAC obligations.
