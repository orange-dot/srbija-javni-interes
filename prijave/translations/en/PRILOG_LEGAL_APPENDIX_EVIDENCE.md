#### Key Findings

### EVIDENCE 3: Balkan Insight Investigation - Deinstitutionalization

#### Bibliographic Reference
- **Title:** 'Worst Nightmare': Serbia's Failure to Deinstitutionalise People with Disabilities
- **Publisher:** Balkan Insight (BIRN - Balkan Investigative Reporting Network)
- **Publication Date:** August 25, 2025
- **Type:** Field investigative journalism (5 journalists)
- **URL:** https://balkaninsight.com/2025/08/25/worst-nightmare-serbias-failure-to-deinstitutionalise-people-with-disabilities/
- **Authors:** Jovanka Nikolic, Dragana Prica Kovacevic, Ivana Jovanovic, Radmilo Markovic, Gordana Andric
- **Access:** Publicly available
- **Language:** English

#### Key Findings
1. **14,512 persons** remain in state institutions despite deinstitutionalization policy
2. **8,617 persons** transferred to private institutions (without real reform)
3. **Only 27 persons** with disabilities use community services (out of 23,000+ who should)
4. **Only 4 of 145 municipalities** have supported living services
5. **Government is disinterested** due to fear of "job losses"

#### Direct Quotations for Court

**For COMPLAINT_I_CORRUPTION (Section IV.A.3):**
> "Despite the Government's 2022 Strategy for Deinstitutionalisation, 14,512 people remain in state institutions and 8,617 in private facilities. Only 27 individuals with disabilities are using community-based services out of an estimated 23,000 who require support." (Balkan Insight, 2025)

**For COMPLAINT_III_POLITICAL_RESPONSIBILITY (Section VII.A.1):**
> "The Government committed to deinstitutionalisation since 2017, yet after 8 years, no meaningful progress has been made. The 2022 Strategy acknowledged that 40% of institutional residents could live independently in the community, but implementation has stalled." (Balkan Insight, 2025)

**For COMPLAINT_IV_EU_FUNDS_INTERNATIONAL (Section 4.A.1):**
> "EU funding for social services remains unimplemented. The Government has systematically shifted budgets from community-based services to private institutional placements, which continues institutionalization under different ownership." (Balkan Insight, 2025)
